What is Break even?

Break-even refers to the point at which total revenue equals total costs, resulting in neither profit nor loss. In other words, it is the point at which a business or project covers all its expenses and starts generating a profit. At the break-even point, there is no net income or net loss.

Example

Sarah starts a new caf√© and determines that she needs to sell 100 cups of coffee per day to reach her break-even point. In the initial weeks, she struggles to attract enough customers and sells an average of only 50 cups per day, resulting in losses. To improve her chances of breaking even, Sarah introduces daily specials and extends her opening hours to accommodate a wider range of customers. She also partners with local businesses to offer discounts and promotions to their employees. These efforts gradually attract more customers, and by the end of the third month, Sarah reaches her break-even point, selling an average of 100 cups of coffee per day.
